Weasel coffee
Weasel dung coffee with wonderful flavor

Weasel coffee, also known as Weasel dung coffee, is a specialty coffee that is considered the world's rarest beverage. This coffee is widely grown in Indonesia, particularly on the islands of Sumatra, Java, and Sulawesi. The term "Kopi Luwak" refers to a sort of nut produced by a spotted civet that consumes coffee berries before excreting, drying, and processing them. The term Kopi Luwak is derived from the Indonesian word kopi, which signifies coffee. Luwak is the name of a region in the Indonesian island of Java, as well as a civet that lives there. Spotted civets may be found across Southeast Asia, including Indonesia, the Philippines, Vietnam, and southern China. Civets are raised in Vietnam to create this unique coffee.
