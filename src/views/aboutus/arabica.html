Arabica coffee
Coffee shrub of Arabia

Arabian coffee, "coffee shrub of Arabia," "mountain coffee," and "arabica coffee" are all names for Coffea arabica. According to two-part nomenclature, it has the following scientific name: Because this species has tiny leaves and morphological traits similar to tea tree, a well-known industrial plant in Vietnam, Coffea arabica was chosen. Moka coffee and Catimor coffee are two forms of tea coffee. Catimor has a robust scent but a little sour flavor, and Moka has a seductive perfume but a pale flavor. It's thought to be the most economically valuable coffee species. About 61 percent of all coffee products are made from coffea. Arabica coffee is also known as Brazilian Milds if it originates in Brazil, Colombian Milds if it originates in Colombia, and Other Milds if it originates in other nations.
