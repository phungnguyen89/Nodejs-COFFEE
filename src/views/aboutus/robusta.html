Robusta coffee
Coffea Robusta or Coffea Canephora

Vietnam exports the most robusta coffee in the world. This type of coffee thrives in most coffee-growing countries because to good factors such as climate, rainfall, and altitude. Coffea Robusta or Coffea Canephora is the scientific name for Robusta. Coffee beans contain 3-4 percent caffeine, whereas coffea has just 1-1.9 percent.

Robusta coffee is undervalued since it contains more caffeine and has a less sour flavor than Arabica coffee. The scent is fairly strong, and the flavor ranges from neutral to quite abrasive, with a flavor that is often characterized as resembling oatmeal. It smells like new peanuts when you smell unroasted Robusta beans. Pure roasted Robusta seeds, on the other hand, have a slight scent upon sniffing them. It smells like burnt rubber if you sniff it closely.
