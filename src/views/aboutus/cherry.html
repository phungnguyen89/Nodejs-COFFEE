Cherry coffee
Coffea liberica, Coffea Excelsa

The Herbaceous family includes cherry coffee and Liberian coffee (two-part nomenclature: Coffea liberica, synonym Coffea Excelsa). Trees range in height from 2 to 5 meters. Unlike Robusta coffee, the stems, leaves, and fruits are all rather huge. They resemble jackfruit trees because of their enormous, dark green leaves. Because the trees are drought resilient and require minimal watering, they are commonly planted in large quantities. Cherry coffee, on the other hand, is not widely consumed and does not have a large growing area due to its low production and sour flavor. Crops are developed largely in specific provinces in Vietnam, such as Nghe An, Quang Tri, Gia Lai, and Kontuam, which have acceptable circumstances for the growth of industrial crops but are not totally favorable for the growth of coffee. This is also why Daklak and, in particular, Buon Ma Thuot are known as the coffee capitals, despite the fact that there is relatively little land suitable for growing this variety of coffee.
